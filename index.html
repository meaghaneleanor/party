Key Factors in Choosing Tutoring Support

  • 5 IMPORTANT QUESTIONS TO ASK A TUTORING COMPANY
  • 3 KEYS TO SUCCESSFUL ACT AND SAT PREP

1. What are your score results?

One of the great things about test preparation for exams like the SAT and ACT is that there is an objective third party measuring stick. A student has one score before the program, and another score after the program. Given this, it should raise flags if a company can't or won't state their average score improvement data, or tries to claim things like, "We only work with high scoring students so it's not a fair comparison." We're proud of our results, and we believe being transparent about those results is the right way to do business.

2. What is your guarantee if we don't see meaningful improvements?

If you buy an iPhone and it doesn't work properly, you take it back to the store and they make it right. That's what ethical companies do, and in our industry that means standing behind promises of score improvement with a guarantee. In our case, if your child doesn't achieve the target score improvement for the program you choose, we will provide you with a full review program for a subsequent exam for free. We know that despite everyone's best efforts, things don't always go perfectly on test day. But we also believe that if a company is willing to take your investment, they should also be willing to stand behind their products and their results.

3. Do you develop your own test preparation curricula?

This may sound like a technical or obscure question, but it's critically important. By law a company that uses independent contractors cannot provide those tutor-contractors with any training, with any ongoing supervision and coaching, or with any form of required curriculum or programming. In recent years a number of companies have tried to cut costs by using contractors while trying to make it sound like their tutors are carefully screened and trained. This question will ensure you know who is who.

4. Are your tutors employees or independent contractors?

5. Do you only do tutoring, or do you also offer small-group and classroom instruction?

Organizations can only be good at so many things, and too often we see companies that claim to be the best at everything. Since our inception we've focused exclusively on one-to-one private tutoring, and as a result our entire operation is built around delivering strong tutoring programs. We have processes and dashboards that enable our Program Directors to monitor each student's progress, curricula and learning technologies that have been custom designed for the one-to-one tutoring dynamic, and recruiting and training processes focused on finding and training great tutors. We might not be very good at classroom-style test preparation programs, but we are experts at delivering high-quality private tutoring.

1. Strategies

Every student should learn the classic test taking strategies that are unique to the SAT and ACT. On standardized exams it doesn’t matter how you arrive at the correct answer, only that you do so. The classic test taking strategies like Backsolving and Substitution are essential to achieving that goal, which is why StudyPoint students spend hours learning and practicing these techniques and many others.

2. Content

The second pillar of test preparation is content review. Since both the SAT and ACT include content spanning your child’s entire education, it may be years since your child has reviewed some of the concepts covered on the exam. For this reason a core part of our program is a thorough review of the exam content. Your child’s tutor will review those areas where he or she needs the most work and assign homework to specifically address content and skill gaps.

3. Familiarity & Practice

Last, and perhaps most important, are practice and familiarity. The SAT and ACT are unlike any other exam that your child will take. That’s one of the reasons they can be so intimidating. Our goal is to build so much familiarity with the test that your child enters the exam room confident and relaxed. Towards this end, StudyPoint students complete a minimum of three full-length practice exams, as well as 12-18 hours of homework over the duration of their programs.
